UCaaS Software

Work Type:Product Management, Design
Date: 7.2018 - 2.2020
Company:Comcast Business

As a Product Designer at Think Company, I worked with Comcast Business full-time. I led the efforts to design and release a Unified Communications as a Service (UCaaS) app for desktop and mobile devices. Allowing customers to utilize their business numbers from any phone, as well as their computer, brought flexibility to the lives of business owners. With Chrome extensions, Alexa integrations, and conferencing features, customers could confidently manage customers and employees from home while continuing to use their business landline number.

During the beta release, I was hired full-time as a Product Owner to the Comcast Business team that built the software. The work below represents my time on the project from Designer to Product Owner.

Role Overview

After transitioning from Designer to Product Owner, I had the opportunity to improve the cross-functional collaboration and development life cycle.

Objectives:

  • Improve cross-functional communication between development and design
  • Improve acceptance criteria in user stories related to design work
  • Support developers and QA in identifying acceptance criteria discrepancies

UCaaS Application Designs

LEAD PRODUCT DESIGNER

With a small team, we set out to deliver desktop and mobile designs with Sketch and Zeplin. During our work, we improved on the process for stakeholder meetings and communication with the product team through better documentation, agendas, and setting expectations for deliverables. The results were more screens delivered, less design adjustments in development, and for me, the opportunity to work as a PO.

Documentation, Analytics, & the Backlog

PRODUCT OWNER

After my transition from Designer to PO, we increased cross-functional communication, implemented a documentation process, reported on OKRs and KPIs, and created design-related defect suites. Supporting development and QA in identifying and mitigating design discrepancies greatly improved each sprint and the development life cycle. Quantitative and qualitative data readouts were regularly presented to the company in order to better prioritize customer needs.
