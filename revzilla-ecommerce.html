Site Redesign Strategy

PRODUCT DESIGN STRATEGIST

The redesign was also a replatform from python to elixir. In order to create a site that could handle frequent content additions, merchandising and planning, and mobile devices, a component-based architecture was proposed and approved. Leveraging components allowed for easily repeatable asset creation and also required the use of a content management system.

Content Management System

PRODUCT OWNER & UX LEAD

Once the company chose to move forward with a component-based site structure, we turned our focus to building an in-house CMS. All requirements were written from a MVP perspective, and the developers leveraged a UI kit to lighten the load on the visual design team as they continued with the sitewide redesign.

Cart & Checkout

PRODUCT OWNER & UX LEAD

The longest running product life-cycle was the iterative redesign of cart and checkout. Accounting for the current structure and functionality of the legacy site, 3 iterations were planned in order to meet the final vision. The last iteration and feature enhancement of shipment groups was implemented 6 months after I left the role, still leveraging all designs and requirements that were created during my time.
