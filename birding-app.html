Birding App

Work Type:Mobile Concept, Machine Learning
Date: 7.2016 & 3.2020
Company:Audubon

In 2016, the proposed challenge was to create a mobile app for the Audubon Christmas Bird Count. The original design expands on this challenge with the goal of creating an app that birders can utilize for annual Audubon events, news, and their own profile for birding life lists. In 2020, an AI / ML product management course consisted of improving business efficiency with ML technology, providing the perfect opportunity to improve the original designs and incorporate automation.

Check out both case studies to see the evolution of the design, learn the history of why Audubon is so important and gain an understanding of how technology can play a role in the business of birding.

2016 App Prototype

For the original project, a video of a mobile prototype was created to show the process of signing in and starting a bird counting session. While much of the design hasn't changed, the 2020 app screens include improved top and bottom navigations, as well as computer vision technology to identify birds the user may not be familiar with.

2016 & 2020 Sitemaps

In 2016, the sitemap was created to organize the app into count sessions and the user profile. The colored circles represent the required screens for the original challenge. The 2020 sitemap streamlines sign in and landing screens and highlights the new feature areas to identify where machine learning technology could be implemented.

2016 & 2020 Userflows

The 2016 userflow steps through the basic process of starting a count session, counting a bird, and ending the session. The 2020 userflows show the steps for utilizing the machine learning technologies: Intelligent character recognition is leveraged for uploading handwritten life lists to databses and computer vision is leveraged to count birds that users may not be able to identify.

App Screens

The first two images in the gallery represent 2016 app screen designs for signing in and counting birds. In the 2020 designs, you'll notice the top and bottom navigations have been improved, computer vision has been added as an option while counting birds, and a Summary section has been created for users to upload handwritten lists, which are very common in the birding community.
